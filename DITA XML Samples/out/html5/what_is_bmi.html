What is BMI ?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple and widely used method to estimate an individual's body fat based on their weight with respect to their height. It’s commonly used by healthcare professionals to evaluate the risk of developing health conditions related to body weight, such as obesity, diabetes, hypertension, cardiovascular diseases, and certain types of cancer. It provides numerical values that are catergorized into different ranges, indicating whether they are underweight,normal weight, overweight, or obese.

  • Underweight: BMI < 18.5
  • Normal Weight: BMI 18.5–24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25–29.9
  • Obese: BMI >= 30

Formula to calculate BMI:

BMI (standard units) = Weight (kg)/Height (m)sq

BMI (imperial units) = Weight (lbs)*703/Height (in)sq
